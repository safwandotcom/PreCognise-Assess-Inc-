### PreCognise Assessment Platform — June 2026

## Executive Summary

PreCognise is a B2B SaaS assessment platform built for high-volume hiring. It enables employers to run fully proctored, scored assessments for up to 15,000 candidates simultaneously — with real-time anti-cheat, speed-bonus scoring, psychometric analytics, and white-label branding.

The platform is **built and deployed.** There is no product risk. The opportunity is distribution.

**Target market:** Mid-to-large employers in banking, BPO, government, insurance, and telecom running annual or quarterly mass hiring rounds.

**Revenue model:** Monthly SaaS subscription per employer, tiered by candidate volume.

**Year 1 target:** 5 paying clients at $2,000/month average = $120,000 ARR
**Year 3 target:** 40 paying clients at $3,000/month average = $1,440,000 ARR

---

## The Problem We Solve

Every large employer faces the same bottleneck: how do you screen 15,000 applicants efficiently, fairly, and with integrity?

The current options are:
1. **Manual written tests** — Logistics nightmare. Printing, venues, invigilators, manual marking. Cost: $15–50/candidate. Scales to hundreds, not thousands.
2. **Whiteboard platforms (Google Forms, Typeform)** — No proctoring, no timing, no scoring. Candidates cheat freely.
3. **Enterprise assessment vendors (SHL, Mettl, Talview)** — Expensive ($3k–$50k/year), opaque scoring, no customisation without a sales cycle, built for ongoing subscriptions not mass events.
4. **Build in-house** — RBC, HSBC, and others have tried. Cost: $200k–$500k for a fraction of the features. Maintenance ongoing.

PreCognise sits between "too basic to trust" and "too expensive to justify." It's the professional-grade tool priced for employers who can't negotiate a six-figure enterprise contract.

| Tier | Candidate Cap per Session | Sessions/Month | Price |
|------|--------------------------|----------------|-------|
| Starter | Up to 500 | 2 | $500/month |

| Enterprise | Up to 15,000 | Unlimited | $4,500/month |
| Enterprise+ | 15,000+ (custom) | Unlimited | Custom from $7,000/month |

**Annual discount:** 2 months free (16% off) for annual upfront commitment

**Add-ons:**
- White-label domain: +$200/month
- Dedicated CSM onboarding: +$500 one-time
- API access for ATS integration: +$300/month
- Custom question type development: quoted separately

### Why Subscriptions, Not Per-Candidate

Per-candidate pricing creates friction at the moment of highest intent (when the client wants to run a big exam). Subscriptions create predictable revenue and let clients run smaller pilot sessions freely before large events. The client relationship becomes long-term rather than transactional.

## Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Land with One Anchor Client (Months 1–3)

You already have RBC as a reference point. Use the existing deployment as a live case study. Approach 2–3 banks or financial institutions directly.

**The pitch:** "We ran 15,000 candidates through this platform at RBC. Here's what the dashboard looks like. Here's what the analytics told their HR team. Here's the anti-cheat log from the last exam. Do you want us to set this up for your next intake?"

This is a product demo backed by real event data — not a slideshow.

**Target: 2 paying clients by end of Month 3.**

### Phase 2: Vertical Expansion (Months 3–9)

Once two clients are live, use their logos and referrals to approach:
- Two more banks in the same region
- Two BPOs (target their HR heads at industry events)
- One government agency (longer sales cycle, but large contract value)

Each new client is a separate deployment (15 minutes of setup), generating $1,500–$4,500/month.

**Target: 8–10 paying clients by Month 9.**

### Phase 3: Scale with a Sales Motion (Months 9–18)

Hire one business development person. Their job: warm outbound to HR Directors using the case study bank. Target: 2 new clients per month.

Simultaneously begin multi-tenant development to improve unit economics on the infrastructure side.

**Target: 20+ clients by Month 18.**

### Channels
1. **Direct outreach to HR Directors** — LinkedIn, referrals from existing clients
2. **HR industry events** — SHRM, local HR associations, banking HR conferences
3. **Recruitment agency partnerships** — Agencies that want to offer assessment as a service to their employer clients
4. **Inbound from case studies** — Publish a blog post: "How we ran 15,000 candidates through a proctored exam in 90 minutes"
5. **Government RFPs** — Longer cycle but recurring, multi-year contracts

## What Could Go Wrong (Risk Register)

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Client runs exam, platform goes down | Low | Very High | Load testing before each large event; Vercel/Neon SLAs; socket server 2 replicas |
| Candidate data breach | Low | Very High | JWT-only auth for candidates (no passwords in transit); bcrypt hashing; Clerk for admins |
| Competitor copies the real-time anti-cheat | Medium | Medium | Speed to market; relationships; roadmap lead |
| Client churns after one exam cycle | Medium | Medium | Annual contracts with 2-month discount incentive |
| Sales cycle longer than expected | High | Medium | Start with warm introductions from RBC; pilot pricing |
| Government/regulated sector procurement delay | High | Low | Avoid government as early clients; come back in Year 2 |
| Multi-tenant development takes too long | Medium | Medium | Keep per-deployment model until Year 2 revenue justifies investment |
